Google Summer of Code has always been one of those opportunities that feels out of reach—until you decide to seriously prepare for it. This journey wasn’t just about getting selected; it was about learning in public, building consistently, and growing with a community.

What made this experience even more meaningful was not doing it alone.

## The Beginning

Back in December, we started **Dev Weekends GSOC Prep** with a simple idea: create a free, structured, and supportive environment for anyone aiming for GSoC.

There was no barrier to entry. Anyone could join, learn, and contribute.

We used:

* **Discord** for discussions, doubt-solving, and collaboration
* **WhatsApp** for quick updates and staying accountable
* **YouTube** for recorded sessions and walkthroughs

But tools weren’t the main thing—**consistency was**.

## How We Prepared

Instead of focusing only on theory, we emphasized practical learning:

* Understanding how open source ecosystems work
* Learning Git, GitHub, and contribution workflows
* Reading and navigating large codebases
* Writing meaningful pull requests
* Communicating with maintainers effectively

Every **Sunday**, we held live sessions. These weren’t just lectures—they were interactive meetups where everyone shared progress, asked questions, and learned together.

Some sessions were messy. Some were slow. But all of them were real.

## The Community Impact

Over time, the group became more than just a prep batch—it became a support system.

People helped each other debug code, reviewed PRs, shared resources, and motivated one another during rejections.

And recently, the results spoke for themselves:

 **10–15 people from our community got selected in GSoC**

That number represents:

* months of consistent effort
* countless small contributions
* failures that turned into learning moments
* growth from beginners to confident contributors

## My GSoC Experience

During my GSoC journey, I worked on real-world problems inside a large open source project. It pushed me to:

* Write clean, maintainable code
* Understand production-level systems
* Collaborate with experienced developers
* Accept feedback and improve quickly

It wasn’t always easy, but it was always worth it.

## What We Learned

The biggest lesson from this journey:

**Consistency matters more than speed.**

You don’t need to rush. You don’t need to compare yourself with others.

What matters is:

* showing up regularly
* putting in honest effort
* learning from mistakes
* staying patient

## What’s Next

Dev Weekends is just getting started.

We plan to continue:

* helping beginners get into open source
* organizing weekly sessions
* building real-world projects
* preparing future GSoC aspirants

Because this was never just about selections.

It was about building a culture of learning and growth.

## Takeaway

Consistency > speed.

Stay patient. Keep building. Show up every week.

That’s how real progress happens.
