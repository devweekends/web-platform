A weekend where 55 builders came together for real engineering, ICPC prep, GSOC journeys, and focused tech circles
## What we worked on
Some weekends feel different.

Not louder.  
Not more hyped.  
Just *real*.

DevWeekends Tech Grind started as a simple idea —  
what happens if people stop waiting for motivation and just **show up to build**?

That weekend, 55 builders showed up.

And something shifted.



![Weekend Tech Grind](/images/post2.jpg)


The goal was never an event.

It wasn’t a workshop.  
It wasn’t a conference.  
It wasn’t another motivational tech meetup.

It was meant to feel like a **shared engineering workspace**.

No stage.  
No spectators.  
Only builders.

Everyone arrived with one intention:

> Work. Learn. Improve.




By this point, things were no longer simple tutorials. We started thinking in terms of systems, structure, and scalability.

## Challenges

Managing dynamic routes and markdown parsing was harder than expected. The real challenge wasn’t coding — it was designing the flow.

## Final thoughts

Building systems properly early saves time later. Speed comes after clarity.
