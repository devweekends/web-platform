Dev Weekends Club

The Dev Weekends Club will be an official student organization at your university, managed and operated by student ambassadors. The club serves as a hub for tech enthusiasts, providing regular activities, learning opportunities, and career development support. All club operations, events, and activities are handled by dedicated student ambassadors who receive training and support from Dev Weekends.

Club Activities & Programs
  • Weekly Tech Circles: Regular meetups for coding challenges, project sharing, and peer learning
  • Monthly Workshops: Hands-on sessions on web development, cloud computing, DevOps, and AI/ML
  • Hackathons & Competitions: Quarterly hackathons and coding contests
  • Mentor AMA Sessions: Ask-Me-Anything with professionals from Microsoft, Upwork, and leading startups
  • Project Showcases: Regular events with mentor feedback
  • Study Groups: Organized groups for competitive programming, system design, and interview prep
  • Career Workshops: Resume building, interview skills, and networking strategies
  • Industry Visits: Tech company visits and guest lectures from industry leaders
Club Leadership Structure

The club is led by student officers (President, Vice President, Technical Lead, Events Coordinator) who serve as ambassadors. These student leaders are trained and supported by Dev Weekends, receiving access to all Fellowship programs, mentorship, and career support resources.

Ambassador Responsibilities

Student ambassadors handle all club operations including:

  • Organizing weekly tech circles and monthly workshops
  • Coordinating hackathons and coding competitions
  • Facilitating mentor AMA sessions and career workshops
  • Managing study groups and project showcases
  • Building community engagement and membership
  • Reporting progress and metrics to Dev Weekends
University Support Required
  • Official recognition as a student organization
  • Access to facilities for meetings and events (classrooms, labs, auditoriums)
  • Faculty advisor support (preferably from Computer Science department)
  • Promotion through university channels (newsletters, social media, student portal)

Note: All club activities, events, and operations are completely free. No budget allocation is required from the university.

Partnership Model

Our partnership is completely free with no financial commitment required from the university. We provide all resources, training, mentorship access, and support at zero cost. The partnership focuses on creating value for students through student-led initiatives managed by ambassadors.

All club activities, workshops, mentorship sessions, and career support are provided free of charge. The university's role is to provide official recognition, facility access, and faculty advisor support.

Next Steps

We are excited about partnering with your university to create a thriving tech community. The implementation will be handled entirely by our student ambassadors with minimal core team involvement:

  1. Initial Discussion: If needed, an online meeting can be arranged with university decision makers to answer questions and discuss partnership details
  2. Ambassador Identification: Our ambassadors will identify and recruit student leaders at your university to serve as club officers
  3. Club Registration: Ambassadors will handle all paperwork and registration processes with your student affairs department
  4. Training & Setup: Ambassadors receive training and resources to establish and operate the club independently
  5. Club Launch: Ambassadors organize and execute the official club launch event and initial activities
  6. Ongoing Operations: All club activities, events, and operations are managed by student ambassadors with remote support from Dev Weekends

The core team remains available for strategic discussions if needed, but day-to-day operations are entirely ambassador-led, ensuring the program is sustainable and student-driven.
